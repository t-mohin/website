Religion and Abortion

  • Bahá'í Faith:Abortion, for the purpose of eliminating an unwanted child, and permanent sterilization are generally forbidden to Bahá'ís unless there is some medical reason for it. At present, Bahá'ís are encouraged to decide based on their own conscience in light of general guidance found in Bahá'í writings.
  • Buddhism:There is no single Buddhist view concerning abortion. Some traditional sources, including some Buddhist monastic codes, hold that life begins at conception, and that abortion, which would then involve the deliberate destruction of life, should be rejected
  • Christianity:No explicit prohibition of abortion in either the "Old Testament" or "New Testament" books of the Christian Bible. For early Christians, it was seen as at least as grave as sexual immorality.
